HPC (High-Performance Computing) by simulating compute clusters via multi-threading (2024-2025):

Using Java's multi-threading capabilities to simulate a compute environment, of nodes, to apply kernels onto massive matrixies.

To simulate the environment (using jobs and threads), no variable can be shared between 2 nodes, resulting to the use of serialisation. This lead to a big chain reaction such as: having to split and reassemble (in original order) the matrix, to not exceed Java VM's default memory allocation limit of 1GB, and accounting for high job failure rates (up to 95%).
